# Tone & Style Guide for Speech Design

## The Five Dimensions of Tone

Every speech sits somewhere on each of these spectrums:

| Dimension | Scale |
|-----------|-------|
| **Formality** | Casual ←→ Ceremonial |
| **Energy** | Calm / reflective ←→ High-energy / urgent |
| **Warmth** | Analytical / detached ←→ Personal / empathetic |
| **Authority** | Peer-level / collaborative ←→ Expert / directive |
| **Humor** | None ←→ Frequent, integral to style |

### Quick Presets

| Preset | Formality | Energy | Warmth | Authority | Humor |
|--------|-----------|--------|--------|-----------|-------|
| **CEO Town Hall** | 3 | 3 | 4 | 4 | 2 |
| **Tech Keynote** | 2 | 4 | 3 | 4 | 3 |
| **Board Update** | 5 | 2 | 2 | 5 | 1 |
| **Team Celebration** | 1 | 4 | 5 | 2 | 4 |
| **Conference Panel** | 3 | 3 | 3 | 3 | 3 |
| **Investor Pitch** | 4 | 3 | 2 | 5 | 1 |
| **Commencement** | 4 | 3 | 5 | 3 | 3 |

## Style Elements

### Sentence Length
- **Short** (8-12 words): Creates punch. Use for key messages.
- **Medium** (15-20 words): Workhorse sentences. Carry the argument.
- **Long** (25+ words): Use sparingly. Good for building tension or painting scenes.

**Rule of thumb**: Alternate. Three mediums, one short punch, one long scene-setter.

### Rhetorical Devices

| Device | Example | Best For |
|--------|---------|----------|
| **Anaphora** (repetition at start) | "We will… We will… We will…" | Building momentum |
| **Tricolon** (rule of three) | "Faster, smarter, bolder" | Memorable phrases |
| **Antithesis** (contrast) | "Ask not what your country…" | Defining choices |
| **Epistrophe** (repetition at end) | "…of the people, by the people, for the people" | Emphasis |
| **Anadiplosis** (end → start chain) | "Fear leads to anger. Anger leads to hate." | Logical chains |
| **Chiasmus** (mirror) | "Not a perfect speech, but a speech that's perfect for you" | Elegant turns |

### Transition Phrases

**Between sections:**
- "Now let me shift to…"
- "That brings me to my second point…"
- "But here's where it gets interesting…"
- "So what does this mean in practice?"

**After data/evidence:**
- "And why does that matter?"
- "Let that number sink in."
- "Think about what that means for [audience's world]."

**Before stories:**
- "Let me tell you about…"
- "I'll never forget the day when…"
- "Picture this…"

## Pacing & Timing

### Words per Minute by Style

| Delivery Style | WPM | Notes |
|---------------|-----|-------|
| Conversational | 140-160 | Natural, podcast-like |
| Presentational | 120-140 | Standard keynote pace |
| Dramatic / Deliberate | 100-120 | TED-style, weight on every word |
| High-energy | 160-180 | Rally, product launch |

### Built-in Pauses

Mark pauses in the script with `[PAUSE]` or `[BEAT]`:

- **After a key number**: "Revenue grew 47%. [PAUSE] Let me say that again."
- **Before a reveal**: "[PAUSE] And that's when everything changed."
- **After humor**: Give the laugh room. "[PAUSE]"
- **During audience interaction**: "Raise your hand if… [WAIT]"

### Slide Sync Markers

Use `[SLIDE]` or `[NEXT SLIDE]` to indicate when the speaker should advance:

```
[SLIDE: Title — "The Market Opportunity"]

Good morning, everyone. I want to start with a question…

[SLIDE: Chart — TAM growth]

Look at this curve. Three years ago, we were here. Today…
```

## Language Do's and Don'ts

### Do
- Use active voice ("We built" not "It was built by us")
- Use concrete nouns ("3 customers" not "several stakeholders")
- Use present tense for immediacy ("This changes everything" not "This will change")
- Use "you" and "we" — make it conversational
- Use analogies the audience already understands

### Don't
- Use jargon the audience won't know (or define it first)
- Use passive constructions in key messages
- Stack more than 2 adjectives
- Use hedging language in calls to action ("maybe we could consider…")
- Use clichés without subverting them ("at the end of the day…")

## Cultural & Bilingual Notes

### Code-Switching (e.g., English + Mandarin)
- Use the dominant language for structure
- Switch for: emotional emphasis, cultural proverbs, humor, audience connection
- Signal the switch: "As we say in Chinese, …"
- Keep switches short (1-2 sentences) unless audience is fully bilingual

### Cultural Sensitivity
- Research greeting customs for the event region
- Avoid sports metaphors that don't translate
- Be careful with humor — it's the least portable element
- When in doubt, lead with respect and curiosity
