# Speech Style Guide

## Core Principles

### 1. Write for the Ear, Not the Eye
- Use short sentences (15-20 words max)
- Prefer simple words over complex ones
- Use active voice predominantly
- Include natural pause points (commas, periods, ellipses)

### 2. The Rule of Three
- Group ideas in threes for rhythm and memorability
- "We came, we saw, we conquered"
- Works for lists, arguments, and emotional builds

### 3. Repetition as a Tool
- Anaphora: Repeat opening phrases ("I have a dream...")
- Epistrophe: Repeat closing phrases ("...of the people, by the people, for the people")
- Use sparingly but deliberately for emphasis

## Tone Calibration

### Formal / Executive
- Third-person references where appropriate
- Industry terminology (but defined on first use)
- Measured pace, longer sentences acceptable
- Minimal humor, maximum authority

### Conversational / Keynote
- First-person narrative ("I remember when...")
- Contractions are encouraged
- Rhetorical questions to engage
- Stories and anecdotes as primary evidence

### Inspirational / Motivational
- Second-person address ("You can...")
- Emotional vocabulary
- Building crescendos
- Call-to-action endings

### Technical / Educational
- Clear definitions before deep dives
- Analogies to bridge complex concepts
- "Imagine..." and "Think of it as..." frameworks
- Recap summaries after each major point

## Structural Elements

### Opening Hooks (First 30 Seconds)
1. **The Startling Statistic**: "Every 3 seconds, someone in the world..."
2. **The Personal Story**: "Three years ago, I stood exactly where you are..."
3. **The Provocative Question**: "What if everything you knew about X was wrong?"
4. **The Bold Statement**: "The future of our industry will be decided in the next 18 months."
5. **The Callback**: Reference something that just happened in the room

### Transitions
- Bridge phrases: "Now, here's where it gets interesting..."
- Signposting: "The second lesson I learned was..."
- Callbacks: "Remember that statistic I mentioned? Well..."
- Physical transitions: "[PAUSE] Let me shift gears for a moment."

### Closings
- **The Callback Close**: Return to your opening story/question
- **The Challenge**: "So I'll leave you with this challenge..."
- **The Vision**: Paint a picture of the future
- **The Simple Ask**: One clear action item
- **The Emotional Peak**: Build to the highest emotional moment last

## Formatting Conventions

### Stage Directions
Use brackets for non-verbal cues:
- `[PAUSE]` — deliberate silence (2-3 seconds)
- `[SLOW]` — reduce pace for emphasis
- `[SLIDE: title]` — advance to next slide
- `[LOOK AT AUDIENCE]` — break from notes
- `[GESTURE]` — physical emphasis

### Emphasis Markers
- **Bold** for words to stress vocally
- *Italic* for internal thoughts or asides
- CAPS for critical keywords (use sparingly)
- `—` em dash for dramatic pauses in text

### Timing Markers
- Add `[~2 min]` markers every 2 minutes
- Total time estimate at the top
- Buffer time noted: "Target: 18 min (2 min buffer)"

## Language Patterns

### Power Phrases
- "Here's what I know for certain..."
- "The data tells us one thing, but the story tells us another..."
- "If you remember nothing else from today..."
- "This is the part where everything changes..."

### Phrases to Avoid
- "I think maybe..." (weak hedging)
- "As you all know..." (presumptuous)
- "I'll try to keep this brief..." (undermines your content)
- "Sorry, I'm not a great speaker..." (never apologize for being there)
- "Can everyone hear me?" (test AV beforehand)

### Audience Engagement
- "Raise your hand if..." (physical engagement)
- "Turn to the person next to you and..." (pair engagement)
- "I'd love to hear from you..." (Q&A setup)
- "Take a moment to think about..." (reflective pause)

## Adaptation Guidelines

### For Different Cultures
- Research local communication norms
- Adjust humor style (self-deprecating works globally)
- Be mindful of idioms that don't translate
- Adjust formality level to cultural expectations

### For Virtual Delivery
- Shorter sentences (attention spans are shorter)
- More frequent engagement prompts
- Visual references to shared screen content
- Explicit transitions ("I'm now going to share...")
- Energy level +20% to compensate for screen flatness

### For Panel Discussions
- Prepare 3-5 key soundbites (15-30 seconds each)
- Have a signature story ready
- Prepare graceful pivots: "That's a great point, and it connects to..."
- Know your 'one thing' — the message you must deliver regardless of questions
