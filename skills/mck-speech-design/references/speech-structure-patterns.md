# Speech Structure Patterns

A library of proven speech architectures. Choose the pattern that best fits the speaker's goal and event context.

---

## 1. The Classic Three-Act

| Act | Purpose | Typical Length |
|-----|---------|---------------|
| **Setup** | Hook + context + thesis | 15-20% |
| **Confrontation** | Evidence, stories, tension | 60-70% |
| **Resolution** | Call to action + memorable close | 15-20% |

Best for: Keynotes, motivational talks, TED-style presentations.

---

## 2. Problem → Solution → Benefit

1. **Problem**: Paint the pain vividly (audience nods)
2. **Solution**: Introduce your idea / product / framework
3. **Benefit**: Show the transformed future state

Best for: Product launches, sales pitches, investor presentations.

---

## 3. Situation → Complication → Resolution (SCR)

McKinsey's pyramid principle applied to oral delivery.

1. **Situation**: "Here's where we are today…"
2. **Complication**: "But here's what's changed / what's wrong…"
3. **Resolution**: "Here's what we should do…"

Best for: Board presentations, strategy updates, executive briefings.

---

## 4. The Monroe Motivated Sequence

1. **Attention**: Hook that grabs
2. **Need**: Establish the problem
3. **Satisfaction**: Present the solution
4. **Visualization**: Help audience see the outcome
5. **Action**: Specific call to action

Best for: Persuasive speeches, fundraising, policy advocacy.

---

## 5. The Nested Loop (Story Stack)

Open multiple stories, resolve them in reverse order:

```
Story A opens →
  Story B opens →
    Story C opens + closes (core message)
  Story B closes
Story A closes
```

Best for: Commencement speeches, after-dinner talks, brand storytelling.

---

## 6. The Listicle

"Three lessons I learned…" / "Five principles for…"

- Number stated up front (creates anticipation)
- Each item: claim → evidence → transition
- Final item lands the thesis

Best for: Conference talks, LinkedIn-style thought leadership, short slots.

---

## 7. The Contrast (What Is vs. What Could Be)

Alternate between current reality and aspirational future:

```
What IS → What COULD BE →
What IS → What COULD BE →
What IS → What COULD BE →
NEW BLISS (the transformed state)
```

Nancy Duarte's "Resonate" model. Used by Steve Jobs, MLK.

Best for: Visionary talks, change management, brand launches.

---

## 8. Chronological / Journey

Simply walk through time:

- **Then**: Origin story
- **Now**: Current state
- **Next**: Where we're headed

Best for: Founder stories, annual reviews, milestone celebrations.

---

## Choosing a Pattern

| If the goal is… | Use… |
|-----------------|------|
| Inspire / motivate | Three-Act or Contrast |
| Persuade / sell | Monroe or Problem-Solution-Benefit |
| Inform / update | SCR or Chronological |
| Entertain / connect | Nested Loop or Listicle |
| Strategic recommendation | SCR (McKinsey style) |
